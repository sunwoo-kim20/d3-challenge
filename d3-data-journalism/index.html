Correlations Discovered Between Poverty and Healthcare Coverage, Obesity

Data from the U.S. Census Bureau and the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System suggest that there exist relationships between economic conditions and health issues at the state level. In both pairs of variables, poverty will be assigned as the independent variable. The first such relationship is between poverty (%) and lack of healthcare coverage (%). The data suggests that there is a positive correlation between the two variables.

The second pair of variable explores the relationship between poverty and obesity (%). Similar to the first discovery, there seems to exist a positive correlation between poverty and the levels of obesity in each state. Further research will be necessary to discover systematic ways to help remedy these health disparities experiences by lower income Americans.
